John flips the camera to show the forest. While moving further into the forest he explains the history.

JONATHAN KEYES

You're probably wondering about the murders, and well, they're all unsolved. Same with the people who went missing. But, there are suspicions about some old Oak Park University students who lost it, ran into the forest and haven't left since.

(Pause)

It would make sense though. Back in 1964, some students went missing. A year later, a body turned up belonging to one of the missing students. Missing limbs and seemingly ripped open, as if eaten. Forensics experts found fingerprints and DNA from some of the other students which led some to believe they'd turned into a cannibal cult.

He flips the camera around to show his face in a closeup

JONATHAN KEYES

You ask me? I think it's a load of crap.

(Turning the camera around)

But alas, it's true according to numerous police reports and independent reporters involving themselves in the narrative. Which I guess I'm doing now as well.

JONATHAN KEYES pulls out a file from his bag

JONATHAN KEYES

According to the first police report that was declassified and released to the public. Rachael Collins was walking in the forest with her

(Holding quotation marks in front of the camera)

Best friend Holly Belle. Holly was seen running away from the forest and later called police. She was found with blood on her face, arms and clothing while only having a few minor scratches. She said something attacked her and Rachael Collins. Her body was found cut into pieces and placed on rocks around the forest. Belle was charged with the murder and mutilation of Rachael Collins. She was given life in prison but on the night she was meant to be incarcerated they found her hung by a tree.

A long pause

JONATHAN KEYES

The case was marked solved but neither family had any form of closure.

JONATHAN KEYES ruffles through his bag trying to find something. Eventually he pulls out a new folder and puts the original back.

JONATHAN KEYES

The second case was of a trucker who took a quick rest near the forest. The report says that there was an engine fault with the truck and the main brakes were deteriorated. The report continues saying that they walked into the forest. Witnesses came forward saying they heard screaming and cries for help on the day of the disappearance. There was no body found. Not even a record of the trip had been recorded. The trucker was never identified. Some believe that they used to lived near Oak Park University. Sends a shiver down my spine just thinking about it.
